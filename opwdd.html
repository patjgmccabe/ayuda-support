OPWDD Questions

Answers to frequently asked questions about OPWDD services.

OPWDD stands for the Office for People With Developmental Disabilities. It is a New York State agency that provides services and supports to individuals with intellectual and developmental disabilities.
Individuals with intellectual or developmental disabilities, such as autism, cerebral palsy, Down syndrome, or other neurological conditions that originated before age 22, may be eligible for OPWDD services.
Self-Direction is a service model under OPWDD that allows individuals to have more control over the services they receive. You can choose your own staff, set schedules, and manage a personalized budget with the help of a Support Broker and Fiscal Intermediary.
A Support Broker is a trained professional who helps individuals and families navigate the self-directed services system. They assist with budgeting, hiring staff, coordinating services, and ensuring the individual's goals and needs are met.
A Fiscal Intermediary (FI) is an agency that helps manage the financial aspects of self-directed services. They handle payroll, billing, and ensure compliance with state regulations on behalf of the individual.
To apply, you or your family can contact your local OPWDD Developmental Disabilities Regional Office (DDRO). They will guide you through the eligibility determination process and connect you with available services.
